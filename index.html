Wallflower

I come from what society would call a "normal family" with my mom and dad, who were married for twenty-six years until my mom sadly passed away four years ago. I am the youngest in the family with an older brother from my dad's previous relationship and an older sister and brother. Growing up in a Christian household, I am grateful for God who has been the center of my existence. It's through faith that I find my identity and purpose. Despite the stereotypes about being the youngest in the family, I admit that some ring true as I've grown older.

I have always cherished our family travels as they instilled in me a deep love for being family-oriented. Regardless of the destination or activity, it's the togetherness I treasure the most. Yet, behind this seemingly ideal family picture lies a feeling of being overlooked - like a wallflower that is only now starting to bloom. Being the youngest in an uneven family dynamic inevitably led to feelings of being overshadowed by my siblings.My sister had my mom all to herself as the oldest, and it almost seemed as though she had more years with our mom before my brother and I came along. Additionally, her appearance made it easier for others to socialize with her compared to me. In contrast, my brother, as the only boy, had the attention of both my mom and dad. He excelled as the star athlete in our family, garnering praise and admiration from those around us. As an observer, it felt like everyone always talked about him to family, friends, and coworkers, fostering a sense of pride and recognition that I often longed for. I used to strive for perfection to be seen, to be acknowledged for who I truly am, not living under the shadow of others, but struggling with the facade it created. I have discovered different facets of my personality - sometimes outgoing, encouraging, and at other times reserved, guarding myself against potential rejection. However, over the past two years, I've journeyed towards self-acceptance, embracing my true self, and shedding the fear of judgment.

I am grateful for my life and the experiences that have shaped me, even though I don't always express my gratitude to God as much as I should. Life's challenges and victories are all part of a divine plan to bring glory to His name. My journey is not about me but a testimony of God's character for those who may feel lost, need encouragement, or seek self-acceptance. Every woman has the potential to bloom into who she was created to be, regardless of past circumstances. So, the obstacles I encounter and navigate are not solely for my benefit but also for young girls who may resonate with my struggles, needing reassurance of their worth and purpose. It's also for the perfectionist teenagers yearning for affirmation and those burdened by self-imposed limitations in their twenties. Through my journey of unlearning, self-love, and embracing my identity in God, I strive to show that every woman can blossom into her true self, transcending past and present circumstances.
